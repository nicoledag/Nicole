Avatar


I created an avatar by manipulating a portrait photo of Zoe Kravitz. I choose this model because I felt that she has features that resemble the main female character in the Avatar movie. By adding numerous effects I was able to transform the photo into an Avatar.

Photomontage


This photomontage is intended to show potential impacts to wildlife and the environment from oil drilling in sensitive wildlife areas. I attempted to respectfully hijack a Jackson Pollock canvas, titled One: Number 31 (1950), by utilizing the artist’s ground-breaking style and technique to show potential impacts from oil spills.

About Me

I am Nicole D’Agnenica, a freelance graphic artist & web developer based in Queens NY, with a passion for art and technology. My work experience includes over eight years in product development, calendar managment, branding, design and color for a major retailer in New York City. 



In 2018 I completed a digital art & design online course at S.U.N.Y. Empire State College and an Intensive Web development certificate course at Codecademy which gave me the tools to create projects such as those shown in the portfolio section on this website, in addition to my own portfolio website.

 

I continue to enjoy developing my skills in this exciting environment of digital arts & and web development. I am currently enrolled in a tech degree program to gain knowledge of advanced webdesign using javascript, jquery, bootstrap, etc.
